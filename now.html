Now | June 2026

What has my
attention.

Mid-June on the Gulf. The water is warm enough to swim and the city has settled into that slow summer rhythm I have come to appreciate. I am forty-plus clients deep in south Texas IT and most days that is exactly enough. What I am working through right now is what comes after enough, and whether I am building toward it or just staying busy.

In progress.

Systematizing. I have been doing this work long enough to see the patterns, and I am starting to document them. Not for a course, not for a product. Just because good systems survive and ad-hoc work does not. I am building the frameworks I wish I had three years ago, the kind of documentation that lets a business run instead of just run around.

Also thinking harder about what I want the next version of this work to look like. The client count is one measure. The quality of the relationships, the complexity of the problems, the kind of infrastructure I am trusted to design, those matter more. Working on closing that gap between what I do and what I want to be known for doing.

Paying attention to.

The Corpus Christi port expansion. The city is positioning itself as a logistics hub and the decisions being made right now will shape the local economy for a generation. I am watching who is at the table, what gets built, and whether the people who actually live here end up better off for it.

How the Gulf South gets written about versus what it actually is. Most travel and culture writing about this part of the country is thin, or wrong, or aimed at people passing through. What I know from living here does not match that picture, and that gap keeps pulling at me.

More on who I am and how I think on the About page.

Summer on the coast. The current runs both ways.
